NRI Passport Guidelines - Special Rules for Non-Resident Indians

Who is an NRI?
A Non-Resident Indian (NRI) is an Indian citizen who resides outside India for employment, business, education, or any other purpose. NRIs hold Indian passports and are Indian nationals.

Passport Services for NRIs
NRIs can avail passport services through:
1. Indian Missions/Embassies/Consulates abroad
2. Passport Seva Kendras in India (during visits to India)

Applying for Passport Abroad (Through Indian Mission)
- Visit the website of the nearest Indian Embassy/Consulate
- Download and fill the passport application form
- Submit at the Indian Mission with required documents
- Processing time varies by mission (typically 4-6 weeks)
- Fee is collected in local currency equivalent

Documents Required for NRIs
1. Current passport (original + copies)
2. Valid visa / residence permit of the country of residence
3. Proof of Indian citizenship (if first-time applicant)
4. Address proof in India (permanent address for passport records)
5. Address proof abroad (utility bill, bank statement, etc.)
6. Photographs as per Indian passport specifications
7. For minors: birth certificate + parents' passports

Re-issue of Passport for NRIs
- Can be done at the nearest Indian Mission
- Old passport must be surrendered
- For lost passports abroad: file a police report in the country of residence + report to Indian Mission
- Emergency Certificate can be issued for immediate travel back to India

Special Rules
- NRIs can use both Indian and foreign address on passport application
- Police verification is done at the permanent address in India
- Tatkal service is generally NOT available at Indian Missions abroad
- Processing time at missions is longer than in India
- OCI (Overseas Citizen of India) card holders are NOT Indian citizens and cannot hold Indian passports
