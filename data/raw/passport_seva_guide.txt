Passport Seva - Complete Application Guide

PART 1: FRESH PASSPORT APPLICATION

1.1 Who Can Apply for a Fresh Passport?
Any Indian citizen can apply for a fresh passport. There is no minimum age requirement. Parents or legal guardians apply on behalf of minor children.

1.2 Online Application Process
Step 1: Visit passportindia.gov.in and click "New User? Register Now"
Step 2: Create an account using your email ID. Verify your email.
Step 3: Log in and click "Apply for Fresh Passport / Re-issue of Passport"
Step 4: Fill in the application form carefully:
  - Personal Details (name, date of birth, place of birth, gender)
  - Family Details (father's name, mother's name, spouse name if applicable)
  - Present Address (must match your address proof document)
  - Emergency Contact Details
  - Identity & Address Proof Document Details
  - Passport Details (if re-issuing)
Step 5: Self-certify the application
Step 6: Pay the fee online
Step 7: Schedule an appointment at your preferred PSK
Step 8: Print the Application Receipt (ARN - Application Reference Number)

1.3 What Happens at the PSK?
When you visit the PSK on your appointment date:
Counter A: Token generation and initial document check
Counter B: Detailed scrutiny of original documents and photocopies
Counter C: Biometric capture (photograph, fingerprints, signature)
Counter D: Final verification by the Passport Granting Officer

The entire process at the PSK takes approximately 2-3 hours.

1.4 After the PSK Visit
- For Normal applications: Passport is printed and dispatched after police verification (if no adverse report).
- Police verification is conducted by your local police station.
- Passport is dispatched via Speed Post to your registered address.
- You can track dispatch status on passportindia.gov.in.

PART 2: RE-ISSUE OF PASSPORT

2.1 When Do You Need to Re-issue?
- Passport validity expired or expiring within 1 year
- Pages exhausted (no more blank visa pages)
- Change in personal details (name, address, spouse name, etc.)
- Lost or damaged passport
- Change from minor to adult passport (on turning 18)

2.2 Re-issue Application Process
The process is similar to fresh passport application. Key differences:
- Select "Re-issue of Passport" instead of "Fresh Passport"
- You must provide old passport details (number, date of issue, place of issue)
- Submit the original old passport at PSK
- For lost passports: file an FIR first, then submit the FIR copy with your application

2.3 Short Validity Passport
If your passport has less than 6 months validity, many countries will not grant you a visa. It is advisable to apply for re-issue when your passport has 1 year or less validity remaining.

PART 3: TATKAL (URGENT) PASSPORT

3.1 What is Tatkal Passport?
Tatkal scheme allows you to get a passport on an urgent basis, typically within 1-3 working days of applying. It requires additional fees and additional documentation.

3.2 Tatkal Eligibility
Any Indian citizen can apply under Tatkal scheme. You must provide one of the following additional documents as verification:
- Aadhaar card
- Voter ID
- PAN card
- Verification certificate from an authority (Annexure F)
- Any document from the "Proof of Identity" list that has been verified by the issuing authority

3.3 Tatkal Fees (in addition to normal fees)
- For 36-page booklet: Rs. 2,000 additional
- For 60-page booklet: Rs. 2,000 additional
These are IN ADDITION to the normal passport fees.

3.4 Tatkal Processing
- Application is processed on priority basis at the PSK
- Police verification may be done BEFORE passport issuance for Tatkal cases
- Passport is typically dispatched within 1-3 working days
- Post-issuance police verification is also conducted

PART 4: MINOR PASSPORT

4.1 Rules for Minor Passport (Below 18 years)
- Both parents must give consent (signatures on the application)
- If only one parent is applying, an Annexure D or Annexure H may be required
- Minor's birth certificate is mandatory
- School leaving certificate / bonafide certificate may serve as additional proof
- Validity: 5 years or until the minor turns 18, whichever is earlier
- No last page address (since 2017 policy change)

4.2 Single Parent / Divorced Parent Cases
- If parents are divorced: court order granting custody must be submitted
- Annexure D: Declaration by the applying parent and consent of the other parent
- Annexure H: If other parent's consent cannot be obtained
- In case of child born out of wedlock: Annexure G may be required

PART 5: APPOINTMENT BOOKING

5.1 How to Book an Appointment?
After submitting the online application and paying the fee:
- Go to "Schedule Appointment" on the portal
- Select your preferred PSK from the list
- Available dates and time slots will be shown
- Select your preferred slot and confirm
- You will receive an email and SMS confirmation

5.2 Walk-in Appointments
Walk-in appointments are not available at all PSKs. Some RPOs allow walk-in for:
- Senior citizens (65+ years)
- Government officials with identity cards
- Physically challenged individuals
Check with your local PSK for walk-in availability.

5.3 Rescheduling and Cancellation
- You can reschedule your appointment up to 3 times
- Log in → "View Saved/Submitted Applications" → "Schedule Appointment" → Reschedule
- Applications not acted upon within 3 months of payment are automatically closed
